Please help the SJSU Beethoven Center acquire a unique, autographed score of Beethoven’s Seventh Symphony! This significant Beethoven treasure is an extremely important historical discovery: a first edition score of the Seventh Symphony, inscribed by Beethoven to Antonie Brentano, the woman some believe was his “Immortal Beloved.”

This score was completely unknown until the owner contacted the Beethoven Center last fall. The inscription has been authenticated by Dr. Michael Ladenburger, former director of the Beethoven-Haus, Bonn. The seller has agreed to offer this exclusively to the Beethoven Center until the end of December.

Beethoven’s handwritten inscription translates as follows: “For my highly esteemed friend Antonie Brentano from the author.” Many scholars believe that Antonie was Beethoven’s Immortal Beloved. Beethoven completed the 7th Symphony in April 1812, just three months before he wrote his famous letters to her.
